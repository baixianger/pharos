Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 19, 2026

Pharos (“the app”) — the macOS app and its iPhone/iPad companion — is a local-first project manager for people working with AI coding agents. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with data.

Data we do not collect

  • Pharos operates no hosted service. Your projects, issues, notes and chats are never sent to us or to any third party.
  • There are no Pharos accounts and no sign-in.
  • There is no analytics, telemetry, crash-reporting or advertising SDK.
  • There is no tracking of any kind.

Where your data lives

All Pharos data — the project registry, issues, progress updates, mesh chat transcripts and attachments — is stored on hardware you control:

  • On the Mac, in the app's local storage (~/Library/Application Support/Pharos), as plain JSON.
  • If you set up a Mesh Broker, on the machine you run that broker on — one of your Macs or your own Linux server. The broker is part of Pharos; it is software you host, not a service we operate.
  • On iPhone/iPad, as a local offline cache of the same data.

To show agent session history, the Mac app reads local session files written by Claude Code and Codex on your machine. These are read locally and never uploaded.

Network activity

By default the Mac app talks only to a broker process on the same machine. If you configure a remote broker or remote hosts, Pharos moves data between your own machines over your private SSH or Tailscale network. Mesh chat messages, attachments and presence information go only to the broker you run. Nothing is routed through Pharos servers — there are none.

Keys and credentials

  • On iPhone/iPad, SSH private keys are generated on the device and stored in the system Keychain, marked device-only — they are never uploaded and never synced to iCloud.
  • On the Mac, passwords used for launching agents on paired machines are read from your login Keychain.

Camera and local network

The iPhone/iPad app asks for camera access only to scan broker pairing codes and to attach photos to mesh messages; photos go only to your own broker. It asks for local-network access to reach your broker and SSH hosts over your private network.

Software updates

The Mac app includes the Sparkle update framework, but it ships disabled — as distributed, it performs no update checks and transmits nothing. If update checking is enabled in a future release, it would fetch release information only, with Sparkle's system profiling off, and this policy will be updated.

Children

Pharos is a developer tool and is not directed at children. It collects nothing from anyone, of any age.

Changes

If this policy changes, the “last updated” date above will change with it.
